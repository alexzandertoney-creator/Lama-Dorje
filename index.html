Buddha's Teachings

The foundation of all Buddhist practice relies on recognizing our current state of mind and developing wisdom and compassion to see things as they truly are.

The Four Noble Truths

These truths serve as a diagnostic map for understanding human dissatisfaction and path to mental freedom:

  1. The Truth of Suffering (Dukkha): Life includes friction, instability, and dissatisfaction.
  2. The Truth of the Cause: Dissatisfaction arises from craving, attachment, and misjudging the nature of reality.
  3. The Truth of Cessation: It is entirely possible to bring an end to craving and attain deep peace (Nirvana).
  4. The Truth of the Path: The way out is accomplished by following the Eightfold Path.

The Noble Eightfold Path

A practical guide divided into three essential aspects of life: Wisdom (Panna), Ethical Conduct (Sila), and Meditation (Samadhi).

  • Right View & Right Intention
  • Right Speech, Right Action, & Right Livelihood
  • Right Effort, Right Mindfulness, & Right Concentration
